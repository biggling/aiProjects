# android-app Research — 2026-03-27_0000

## Play Store Productivity Benchmarks
- **Revenue & Models**: Android productivity ARPU is ~$6.19. 98% of revenue is freemium. Subscriptions are the primary driver, but users are showing "subscription fatigue" with apps like Forest moving to "Plus" tiers.
- **Discovery Threshold**: A 4.5+ star rating is now mandatory for Play Store visibility in 2026. Retention <15% leads to search deprioritization.
- **Agentic AI**: 32% of new productivity features are "Agentic AI"—proactive assistants that automate routines rather than just tracking time.

## AdMob Revenue Benchmarks (Thailand 2026)
- **High-Value Formats**: Rewarded Video ($2.50–$5.50) and Interstitial ($3.50–$4.80) are the top earners in Thailand. Banner remains low at $0.15–$0.35.
- **App Open Ads**: Emerging as a strong performer for utilities with eCPMs of $1.80–$2.50.
- **DAU for $100/mo**: To earn $100/month in Thailand with a blended eCPM of ~$2.50 (mix of interstitial and rewarded), an app needs approximately **400–600 Daily Active Users (DAU)**, assuming 2-3 high-value impressions per user session.

## Competitor App Analysis — Focus/Block Apps
- **Forest (Negative)**: Users are abandoning Forest due to "enshittification" (persistent ads for Pro users, new "Plus" subscription) and broken notifications that defeat the focus purpose.
- **Freedom (Negative)**: High battery drain (10-15%) due to VPN blocking and lack of granular (URL-level) blocking.
- **Opportunity**: A gap exists for a "Physical Friction" integration (NFC tags or "Brick" like features) and better Android-native UI (Live Notifications/Lock screen timers) which are currently iOS-exclusive for many competitors.

## Kotlin + Jetpack Compose Updates
- **Android 16 (API 36)**: Introduced `ProgressStyle` notifications for real-time focus tracking and "Notification Cooldown" to prevent alert spam.
- **Background Services**: Stricter FGS (Foreground Service) rules; developers must prioritize "User-Initiated Data Transfer" APIs.
- **Compose 1.10+**: "Pausable Composition" eliminates jank by spreading UI work across frames. "Strong Skipping Mode" is now default in Kotlin 2.2, reducing recomposition boilerplate.

## Thai Android Market
- **Localization Gap**: There is a high demand for Thai Natural Language Processing (NLP) in productivity tools. Most apps only offer basic translation, failing to handle Thai idioms or cultural nuances.
- **Privacy First**: Thai users are increasingly vocal about data privacy, favoring "Edge AI" (on-device processing) over cloud-based AI harvesting.
- **Top Rising Apps**: PikPak (Cloud Storage) and Easy Notebook are trending in Thailand, showing a preference for simple, fast utility tools.
