# android-app Research — 2026-04-12_deep-dive

## Focus To-Do — User Pain Points

- **Subscription pricing**: Free tier available; Premium costs $1.99/month, $3.99/3-month, or $11.99 lifetime one-time purchase. The lifetime option is actively promoted and exists, meaning the competitor already offers a one-time purchase path. [Source: goalsandprogress.com/is-focus-to-do-worth-it-in-2025-full-review-features-focus-to-do/]
- **Free tier hard limits**: Free users cannot set Pomodoro timer duration beyond 25 minutes; weekly/monthly/yearly analytics are locked behind paywall. This is the most-cited frustration — users want custom timer lengths without paying. [Source: justuseapp.com Focus To-Do reviews 2025]
- **Sync failures**: Multiple 1-star reviews in 2025 describe completed tasks disappearing after sync, "sync function is completely worthless," and PC-to-phone sync not reflecting correctly. Customer service described as "nonexistent." [Source: chrome-stats.com Focus To-Do reviews]
- **Progress resets randomly**: Users with months of streaks/Forest points report data wiping with no recovery path. Affects the gamification retention loop. [Source: chrome-stats.com reviews]
- **Timer stops when app closes**: Background timer behavior is broken for several users — "whenever I closed Focus To-Do the timer stopped, making the whole experience very frustrating." [Source: chrome-stats.com reviews]
- **UI criticised as clunky**: Cross-confirmed with prior research session (2026-04-06). The Material design is dated; reviewers on Slant and Product Hunt call it "overwhelming" for new users.
- **Explicit demand for one-time alternative**: Community roundups (Medium, Zapier, MetaFilter 2025) show recurring user sentiment: "I'd pay once, not monthly." Clockwork Tomato (free, no subscription) and Brain Focus are cited Reddit alternatives. No dominant paid-once Pomodoro app holds the Android market — this gap is real. [Source: redditfavorites.com/android_apps/clockwork-tomato]
- **Install count**: Focus To-Do: Pomodoro & Tasks (com.superelement.pomodoro) has 10M+ installs on Play Store — the market is large and proven. [Source: play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.superelement.pomodoro]

## Money Manager — Thai User Sentiment

- **Top competitor landscape in Thailand**: The finance category in Thailand Play Store is dominated by banking super-apps — K PLUS (Kasikornbank), Paotang (Krungthai), SCB EASY, TrueMoney (50M+ downloads). These are NOT expense trackers; they are banking apps. The personal budgeting/expense-tracking niche is a separate and less crowded sub-category. [Source: appfigures.com/top-apps/google-play/thailand/finance]
- **Thai-developed expense apps already exist**: "Metang" is a Thai-language expense tracker with voice recording, Excel export, credit card reminders, Dropbox/Drive backup, fingerprint auth, and full Thai-language UI. It is the closest Thai-first competitor. Install count not confirmed from search results. [Source: promise.co.th/blog/money-management-apps.html]
- **Other Thai-market apps noted**: Money+ (cute UI, local storage only — no registration), SET Happy Money (by Stock Exchange of Thailand), Piggipo (credit card tracking). Most are free with no clear monetization. [Source: trustfinance.com Thai budget app roundup 2025]
- **Money Manager (Realbyte) privacy issue confirmed**: The app collects and shares "App activity, App info and performance, Device or other IDs" with third parties. Privacy policy explicitly logs advertisement identifiers and service use records. One reviewer flagged: "It needs internet to connect to the PC manager — this does not seem safe to me." A finance app collecting ad identifiers is a legitimate privacy red flag. [Source: realbyteapps.com/policy/privacy.html; chrome-stats.com Money Manager reviews]
- **Ads in free Money Manager**: Confirmed ad-supported free tier. "Remove Ads" paid version exists at ~$5.49 lifetime. Ads in a personal finance app handling sensitive transaction data is a documented complaint vector. [Source: play.google.com Money Manager (Remove Ads) listing]
- **PromptPay volume validates the market**: Thailand's PromptPay processed 2.1 billion transactions totalling 4.43 trillion THB in March 2025 alone. Thai users are highly active in mobile finance — a budget app that integrates with PromptPay QR workflows would align with existing behaviour. [Source: Facebook/The Nation Thailand PromptPay stats]
- **Gap confirmed**: No Thai-built, privacy-first, offline-capable, $2.99 one-time-purchase expense tracker exists that prominently supports THB default, Thai categories, and PromptPay workflow. Metang is the closest but appears free/ad-supported with no clear paid privacy tier.

## Thai Buddhist Meditation — Gap Analysis

- **Thai-language meditation app exists but is thin**: "สมาธิเสบียงบุญ" (Sabiang Boon Meditation) is on both Android and iOS, last updated October 2025. It offers a meditation timer, relaxing music, and merit-collection features tied to royal charity events. It is more a merit-donation app than a meditation training tool. Install count and rating not confirmed from search results. [Source: play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.apps.sabiangboon]
- **No full Thai Buddhist training app found**: Thai-language articles recommending meditation apps (Medium/Pui Sasakorn, mademindday.com) list: Headspace, Insight Timer, Calm — all English-first, all subscription-based. No Thai-language app with dhamma talks + breathing timer + guided chanting exists in any reviewed source.
- **Calm and Headspace subscription costs**: Both price at $69.99/year (~$5.83/month) globally. At current THB rates (~35 THB/USD), this equals ~2,450 THB/year. For Thai context, this is a high discretionary spend. Both apps rate "Poor" on Trustpilot (Calm: 1.8/5 from 227 reviews; Headspace: 2.0/5 from 518 reviews). Top complaints: impossible to cancel, auto-renewal without warning, no refunds. [Source: sbigrowth.com Headspace/Calm pricing teardown; trustpilot.com reviews]
- **Buddhify (closest one-time model)**: Priced at $2.99 Android / $4.99 iOS one-time purchase. Made by a husband-wife indie team (Rohan & Lucy Gunatillake). 200+ guided meditations. Users explicitly praise "no subscription pressure" and "happily pay $5 once." App appears actively maintained (review data from 2024-2025). English-only — zero Thai localization. [Source: choosingtherapy.com/buddhify-app-review/; mindtools.io/programs/buddhify]
- **Plum Village app**: Free, Buddhist, Thich Nhat Hanh lineage, guided meditations in multiple languages — but NOT in Thai, and NOT Theravada/Thai Buddhist tradition. Available Android/iOS. [Source: plumvillage.app/about-the-plum-village-app]
- **Market gap is real**: Thai Theravada Buddhist meditation (the dominant tradition in Thailand) has zero dedicated, quality, Thai-language Android app with offline content. The combination of (a) no Thai alternative, (b) Calm/Headspace subscription backlash, (c) proven Buddhify one-time model at $2.99, and (d) Thailand's 95%+ Buddhist population = a niche but culturally resonant opportunity.
- **Content risk**: Dhamma talks, chanting audio, and meditation scripts require either original content creation or licensing from a temple/teacher. This is a 5 hrs/week developer constraint — content production is the bottleneck, not code.

For Thailand, Thai-script keywords in the title carry significant extra weight vs. English-only competitors. [Source: appradar.com Google Play ASO guide 2025]
- **Productivity category size**: Productivity apps reached 5.93 billion downloads globally in 2025, ranking 3rd after Entertainment and Finance. The category is large but also highly competitive at global level. [Source: apptweak.com app downloads by category]
- **Google's 2025 algorithm shift**: Google moved its ranking signals from install volume to retention. Apps with high Day-7 and Day-30 retention now rank higher. This benefits niche, high-engagement apps over viral-but-churny ones. [Source: asolytics.pro Google Play ASO 2025]

## Paid App Conversion Rates

- **Hard paywall apps (free limited tier + paid unlock)**: Median conversion rate of 12.11% — significantly better than freemium (2.18%). For a $2.99 one-time paid unlock, this is the relevant model. [Source: adapty.io trial conversion rates 2026]
- **Opt-in free trials convert at 18–25%**: If offering a time-limited free trial (e.g., 7 days full access, then $2.99 to keep), opt-in (no card required) trials land 18–25% paid conversion. The best apps hit 60%+. [Source: adapty.io; amraandelma.com free trial statistics 2025]
- **Lower price = higher conversion**: Data confirms that lower price points ($0.99–$2.99) show stronger conversion (47.8%) vs. higher-priced apps (28.4%). $2.99 is in the optimal zone for conversion probability. [Source: adapty.io trial conversion rates 2026]
- **Android users 30–50% less profitable than iOS**: Despite higher download volumes, Android users convert to paid at lower rates and spend less per purchase. This is a structural headwind for any Android-first paid app. [Source: adpushup.com app monetization statistics]
- **Trial timing**: 80–90% of all trial starts happen on Day 0 (first launch). Onboarding quality is the single most important conversion lever — poor onboarding kills paid conversion immediately. [Source: adapty.io]
- **Regional pricing impact**: In-app purchases optimized for local markets convert 3–5x better than at USD prices. For Thailand, Google recommends 40–60% of Tier 1 pricing. At 50% discount, $2.99 USD becomes ~$1.49 locally (approximately 52–55 THB), which is within an impulse-buy range for Thai users. [Source: regionalpricecalculator.com; mirava.io localized pricing guide]
- **Indie developer revenue benchmarks**:
  - Median Android app earns less than $500/month (Sensor Tower data).
  - Top 1% of apps earn $82,000+/month.
  - Indie developers represent only 5–10% of total mobile app revenue.
  - Real-world indie case study (Itemlist, iOS home inventory app, $2.99–$59.99): Black Friday 2023 generated $1,400+ in a single month with a 50% lifetime deal. Most users chose yearly or lifetime over monthly. [Source: dabo.dev/revealing-my-revenue-metrics]
  - A 30-app indie portfolio case study on Indie Hackers reached $22K/month — not a single-app result but demonstrates portfolio approach viability. [Source: indiehackers.com]
- **Realistic target for a solo dev with 5 hrs/week**: Reaching $500/month requires roughly 167 paid downloads/month at $2.99 (after Google's 30% cut = ~$2.09 net). At 12% conversion from free trial installs, this needs ~1,400 free trial installs/month. Achievable with consistent ASO + Thai-language localization driving organic installs.
- **Thailand market Android dominance**: Android holds 67.67% of Thai mobile OS market (June 2024). 65.4 million internet users in Thailand. The audience exists; monetization per user is lower than US/EU but volume compensates with proper regional pricing. [Source: statista.com Thailand mobile OS share]
