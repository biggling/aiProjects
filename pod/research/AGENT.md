## Context
Etsy print-on-demand (POD) store. Selling physical printed goods (t-shirts, mugs, posters, tote bags).
Using Printify or Printful for fulfillment. Target: passive income from evergreen niche designs.
Primary persona focus: Gen X women, Gen Y (Millennials), Gen Z — compare difficulty and opportunity per segment.

## Research Tasks

### 1. Opportunity Analysis — Niche Scoring
Search: "Etsy POD niche opportunity 2026", "print on demand low competition high demand", "best POD niche to enter 2026"
- Which POD niches have high search volume but thin competition right now?
- Rate each candidate niche on: competition / design complexity / price ceiling / reorder potential
- Which niches are evergreen vs. trend-dependent?
- Any POD categories growing faster than t-shirts (e.g. tote bags, wall art, journals, hoodies)?
- What do Etsy sellers say is underserved? (Reddit, Facebook groups)

### 2. Market Size & Growth
Search: "Etsy print on demand market size 2026", "POD industry growth rate 2026", "Etsy active buyers 2026"
- How many active buyers does Etsy have in 2026? Growth trend?
- What's the total POD market size and annual growth rate?
- Which product categories are growing fastest on Etsy?
- What % of Etsy searches are from mobile vs. desktop? (affects design sizing)
- Any reports on average Etsy POD shop monthly revenue?

### 3. Target Persona — Gen X Women (born 1965–1980, age ~45–60)
Search: "Gen X women Etsy shopping 2026", "what does Gen X woman buy on Etsy", "Gen X gift ideas POD"
- What themes resonate with Gen X women? (nostalgia, 80s/90s pop culture, career milestones, "aging gracefully" humor)
- What products do they buy most on Etsy? (gifts for self vs. gifts for others?)
- What price range are they comfortable with ($15-$30, $30-$50)?
- What triggers a Gen X woman to buy a POD item? (gifting occasion, self-expression, humor?)
- How do they discover Etsy products? (Pinterest, Google, Etsy search?)
- **Difficulty rating**: competition level, design style requirements, price sensitivity

### 4. Target Persona — Gen Y / Millennials (born 1981–1996, age ~28–44)
Search: "millennial Etsy shopping behavior 2026", "millennial gift trends POD", "millennial humor merchandise"
- What design styles appeal to Millennials? (minimalist, self-deprecating humor, career/parenting relatable content)
- What life stage content sells? (new parents, homeowners, career burnout, 30s/40s milestones)
- Most popular product types for Millennials on Etsy?
- Are Millennials more likely to buy for themselves or as gifts?
- Willingness to pay premium for quality vs. seeking deals?
- **Difficulty rating**: competition level, design style requirements, price sensitivity

### 5. Target Persona — Gen Z (born 1997–2012, age ~13–28)
Search: "Gen Z Etsy shopping 2026", "Gen Z fashion trends POD", "Gen Z humor merchandise TikTok"
- What aesthetic movements drive Gen Z buying? (dark academia, cottagecore, Y2K, bimbocore, clean girl, etc.)
- Do Gen Z buyers actually purchase on Etsy or just browse? (conversion rate vs. other generations?)
- What price ceiling works for Gen Z (lower disposable income)?
- How much does TikTok/social media virality drive Gen Z POD purchases?
- Which product types index highest for Gen Z (stickers, hoodies, tote bags, phone cases)?
- **Difficulty rating**: trend velocity (hard to keep up), price sensitivity, TikTok dependency

### 6. Persona Difficulty Comparison
Synthesize findings from tasks 3–5 into a comparison table:
- For each persona (Gen X women / Gen Y / Gen Z): Competition level | Design effort | Price ceiling | Discovery channel | Trend stability | Estimated difficulty (Easy/Medium/Hard)
- Which persona is the best starting point given limited design resources?
- Which persona has the best long-term evergreen potential?
- Can one design serve multiple personas, or do they need separate targeting?

- Any seasonal trends coming up in the next 45 days worth prepping for?
- Which trends are specific to one generation vs. cross-generational?

## Decision Triggers — Flag if Found
- Gen X women niche with >1,000 searches and <200 listings → highest priority (easiest segment)
- Gen Z trend going viral on TikTok → fast-follow design within 72 hours or skip
- Millennial life-stage content gap (e.g. "40s humor") with low competition → evergreen opportunity
- Seasonal event in next 45 days with high purchase volume → create designs now

- Persona difficulty comparison shows one segment clearly easier → focus first product line there
